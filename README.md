## HACKING BY THE SEA
- Problem Statement: Starting your studies in the Netherlands means going through a long list of official steps, and most of them are in Dutch and handled by different organisations. The steps also depend on each other. You need a BSN to open a bank account, but to get a BSN you first need an appointment to register your address at the municipality. If you start working, you have to arrange Dutch health insurance within a set time. Students from outside the EU have extra steps for their residence permit that EU students do not have. Nobody gives you the right order, and doing things in the wrong order can cost you weeks and make you miss deadlines. The information is out there, but it is spread around, it depends on your situation, and it assumes you already know how the system works.
- Goal: Build a tool that turns all of this into one clear, ordered checklist that fits the student using it. The interesting part is that the steps depend on each other, so the right order is not the same for everyone. A good tool asks a few short questions, for example whether you are from the EU or not and whether you are going to work, and then shows the right steps in the right order. The main parts are a description of the steps and how they depend on each other, the logic that puts them in the correct order, the questions that adjust the list to the user, and the checklist itself where you can tick things off. Decide as a team how far you want to take it and where you stop.
- Basic requirements: The tool shows a clear, ordered checklist of the steps a new student has to take, with each step explained in simple language and a link to the official page for it. The user can tick steps off and that progress is saved. Even one well- researched standard path, shown clearly and simply, is already very useful.
- Hard requirements: The steps and their order have to be correct, so base them on official sources and link to those sources. The tool gives information and points people to the right place. It must not give immigration or legal advice and it must not replace the official instructions. Keep the language simple, and make sure the checklist works with a keyboard and a screen reader, not only with a mouse. If you add translation or use a language model, run it on your own machine rather than sending student details to an online service.
- User group: International students arriving in Zeeland, and to some extent Dutch students who move out and have to arrange everything by themselves for the first time. The real problem here is a complicated system that assumes you already understand it, which is the same barrier that people with a cognitive or learning disability run into, so a clear and simple path helps them too.
- Inspiration: Topological sort/dependency graph, a simple rules engine, gemeente inschrijving, BSN, Study in NL, IND (for the non-EU steps), clear language, saving progress
